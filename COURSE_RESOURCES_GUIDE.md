# Course Resources Guide

## Guidelines for Additional Resources Sections

When adding "Additional Resources" to lesson files, follow these guidelines:

### ✅ DO:
- **Use direct, specific links** - Link to exact pages, not homepages
- **Verify links work** - Test all links before adding them
- **Choose reputable sources** - Use well-known, trusted websites
- **Be specific** - Link to specific articles, statistics, or tools
- **Provide context** - Explain what the resource is and why it's helpful
- **Use diverse sources** - Mix statistics, articles, tools, and guides

### ❌ DON'T:
- Use placeholder links (like `https://www.statista.com/` without a specific page)
- Link to homepages without context
- Use broken or outdated links
- Add resources that aren't relevant to the lesson
- Use too many resources (3-6 is ideal)

### Resource Types to Include:

1. **Statistics & Data**
   - Statista pages with specific statistics
   - Industry reports
   - Research studies

2. **Helpful Articles**
   - Expert blog posts
   - Industry insights
   - Case studies

3. **Tools & Calculators**
   - Revenue calculators
   - ROI calculators
   - Time management tools

4. **Guides & Tutorials**
   - Step-by-step guides
   - Best practices
   - How-to articles

### Example Format:

```markdown
## Additional Resources

- [Statista: Specific Topic Statistics](https://www.statista.com/specific-page) - Brief description of what this resource provides
- [Forbes: Article Title](https://www.forbes.com/specific-article) - Why this is helpful
- [Tool Name: Calculator](https://tool.com/calculator) - What you can calculate with this
```

### Verified Resource Sources:

- **Statista** - Statistics and market data
- **Forbes** - Business insights and expert opinions
- **Harvard Business Review** - Research and case studies
- **Square** - Small business tools and statistics
- **HubSpot** - Marketing and customer experience data
- **BookingBug** - Appointment booking industry insights
- **Time Doctor** - Productivity and time management data
